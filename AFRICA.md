# The African Legal Corpus

The Open Legal Corpus (OLC) is a pan-African infrastructure project. 

It currently spans **four major regional economic communities**, covering West, Central, East, and Southern Africa.

---

## What This Means

The OLC is the first publicly available, machine-readable, structured corpus of African regional law. 

It exists to serve:

- **Legal professionals** conducting cross-jurisdictional research.
- **Legal technologists** building AI tools that understand African law.
- **Students and academics** studying African jurisprudence.
- **Policymakers** seeking to harmonize regional legal frameworks.

---

## The Vision

The OLC is not a finished product. It is a **living foundation**.

The next phase includes:
- Adding **national legal systems** (Cameroon, Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, etc.).
- Adding **landmark judgments** from regional and national courts.
- Adding **semantic search and embedding tools** for AI-assisted legal research.
